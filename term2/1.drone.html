Drone

Recently I came across the CircuitMess Wheelson on the Arduino website and got the idea to initiate a setup for a Arduino drone. Since there is plenty of information on the topic on numerous blogs, online communities and webshops, creating the concept of the hardware turned out not to be difficult with plenty of references available online.

figure 1: Schematic overview of drone hardware and cicuitry.

Timeline:

  • October 21: Functional test passed of first quarter of the EMC/rotor/motor assembly.
  • December 21: Frame, RC-transmitter and RC-receiver inventarized.
  • March 1: Discussion with Eduardo on initial soft- and hardware setup.

Goal and strategy:

The goal of this project is to get more familiar with electronic hard- and software and control a setup of a drone that can be applied in agritech contexts to scan, monitor and/or intervere in a growing environment. Though there is not a fixed desired outcome of this project I try to create a system that can be changed/adapted to a certain extend. When showing the draft of the circuitry Eduardo suggested to swap the Arduino Uno with a Naze32 flight controlling unit.
